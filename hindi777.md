## 10. Advanced Strategies for Popular Games

These strategies go beyond basic rules. They assume you have already practiced and understand the core mechanics.

### 10.1 Teen Patti – The Minimum Raise Bluff

Instead of raising big when you have a strong hand, raise the **minimum** every time (usually 2x the blind). This does two things:
- Opponents cannot distinguish a bluff from a trail.
- After 3‑4 minimum raises, many players fold out of frustration.

**Risk:** Low – you only lose the minimum raise if called.

### 10.2 Rummy – The “Two Sequence” Trap

Hold one high card (King/Queen) of the same suit as your almost‑complete sequence. Discard a middle card instead. Opponents will think you are struggling and stay in longer, allowing you to collect more when you declare.

**Warning:** Use this only against intermediate players. Experts will see through it.

### 10.3 Andar Bahar – The 3‑Hand Rule

Because Andar Bahar is pure luck, never play more than 3 hands in a row. After 3 hands (win or lose), switch to a different game or take a break. This prevents the “just one more” trap.

### 10.4 Crash Game – Cash Out Early

The most common mistake in Crash Game is waiting for a 5x or 10x multiplier. Statistically, cashing out at **1.5x – 2x** yields the best long‑term results. You will win more often, even if each win is smaller.
